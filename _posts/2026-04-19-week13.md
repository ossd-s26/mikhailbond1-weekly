# The issue with finding issues: 👀
You would think it would be simple - open source projects always have countless parts breaking down and requiring people to fix things. However, it is not as easy as you would think to actually work on one of these issues.

P5.js has a system where, if you are assigned to an issue, you get the right to work on a fix without anyone else beating you to it. The benefits of this are that if you are quick enough to comment and get assigned one, you can focus on building out your solution without racing against the clock. The downside of this is that if you are not fast enough to claim an issue before the flocks of other P5 developers see it, you are out of luck.

# This is a problem with open source development:
There is no good solution to this.

If the project does not assign issues, countless developers spend hours working on a solution just to have another developer beat them to submitting a PR.

If they do assign issues, it is harder to find open issues.

Both have their pros and cons.

# Change in my approach:
To get more work into the P5 project, I am going to change my approach from finding existing issues to creating an issue myself. When you create an issue yourself, you get first dibs on it.
